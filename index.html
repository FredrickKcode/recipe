Traditional Ugandan Foods

Matooke

Ingredients:

  • 8 green plantains (matoke)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

Peel the plantains and place them in a pot with water. Steam until tender. In another pan, heat oil and sauté the onions until golden brown. Add tomatoes, green pepper, and curry powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften. Mash the steamed plantains and mix them with the sautéed ingredients. Add salt to taste and serve hot.

Luwombo

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or beef, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon groundnut paste (peanut butter)
  • 4 large banana leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water

Instructions:

Wilt the banana leaves over low heat until pliable. In a large bowl, mix the chicken or beef with onions, tomatoes, green pepper, garlic, groundnut paste, salt, and pepper. Place the mixture in the center of a banana leaf, fold the leaf to enclose the filling, and tie with a string. Place the wrapped leaf in a pot, add water, cover, and steam for about 2 hours. Serve the stew in the banana leaf with a side of matoke or rice.

Rolex

Ingredients:

  • 2 chapatis
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Instructions:

In a bowl, beat the eggs with salt, then mix in the chopped tomato, onion, and green pepper. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat and pour in the egg mixture to make an omelet. Once the omelet is cooked, place it on a chapati and roll it up. Repeat with the second chapati and serve hot.
